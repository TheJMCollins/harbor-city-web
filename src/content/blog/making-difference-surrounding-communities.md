We are a church that makes a difference in our surrounding communities.

We don't just go to church in the city—we serve the city.

It's possible to love good preaching, enjoy great worship, and still miss the point if the Gospel never leaves the building.

Jesus never intended the Church to be a weekly event people attend. He intended it to be a living presence people feel—right where pain lives, where needs are real, and where hope is in short supply.

> "You are the light of the world... Let your light so shine before men, that they may see your good works and glorify your Father in heaven." (Matthew 5:14, 16, NKJV)

That's not a call to show off. It's a call to show up.

## Why Community Impact Is a Gospel Issue

A lot of people treat "helping the community" like a side ministry—nice if you have the budget and the volunteers.

But biblically, community impact is part of what it means to follow Jesus.

Because the Gospel isn't only a message you believe. It's a life you live.

> "For we are His workmanship, created in Christ Jesus for good works..." (Ephesians 2:10, NKJV)

Saved people do good—naturally, consistently, intentionally—because the life of Christ is in them.

## We Believe the Church Should Be Good News in Practical Form

Faith isn't only about what happens in your prayer closet. It's also about what happens when you step into someone else's world.

Sometimes ministry looks like:

- feeding families
- supporting schools
- helping single parents
- mentoring students
- showing up for neighborhoods
- serving city leaders and first responders
- building bridges across cultures
- bringing excellence into places that have been neglected

And yes—sometimes it means meeting physical needs first, because love speaks loudest in action.

> "Let us not love in word or in tongue, but in deed and in truth." (1 John 3:18, NKJV)

## Why We Start Here: Our Surrounding Communities

We care about the nations—and we will reach the nations.

But we believe in a simple principle: if we won't love the people closest to us, we won't love the world well either.

> "And seek the peace of the city... and pray to the LORD for it; for in its peace you will have peace." (Jeremiah 29:7, NKJV)

God planted Harbor City here on purpose. The neighborhood around us is not an obstacle to our mission—it *is* our mission.

## The Kind of Difference We Want to Make

We're not trying to do random acts of kindness. We want sustained, strategic impact—real transformation over time.

That means our approach is:

- relational, not just transactional
- consistent, not just seasonal
- dignifying, not pity-driven
- collaborative, not competitive
- excellent, not sloppy

Excellence matters when serving people, because people matter to God.

## What This Looks Like for You

Making a difference is for everyone—not just the extroverts or the ultra-available.

**Pray for the city.** Pray for leaders, schools, safety, families, and the spiritual climate of our neighborhoods.

**Serve with your hands.** Show up, help, build, distribute, clean, mentor—whatever the need is.

**Serve with your skills.** Doctors, lawyers, educators, administrators, tradespeople, creatives—your expertise becomes ministry when offered with love.

**Give toward impact.** Generosity fuels outreach. When you give, you're not just funding a church—you're funding change.

## A Final Thought

We don't serve our community because it's trendy.

We serve our community because it's Christian.

Jesus didn't move into neighborhoods to observe problems. He stepped into them to heal, restore, and raise what was broken.

And now He sends His Church to do the same.

Not someday. Not when we're bigger. Not when it's convenient.

Now. Right here.
